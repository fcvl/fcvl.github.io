    Background:燃料电池内部多尺度多相热质传输过程与电化学反应机理高度耦合，涉及上百结构，工况线性关系，使得燃料电池设计过程处于“黑箱状态”。 作为一种新兴的研究方法，机器学习已被燃料电池和其他能源系统社区用于性能预测、寿命预测和故障诊断。 然而，数据收集仍然是主要的挑战，因为多物理场的观测通常需要使用特定和昂贵的仪器； 传统的CFD模型数值模拟虽然能很好吻合实验结果，但是计算成本高，计算时间长。

    Compare: 对于人工神经网络，我们只需要用少量的数据（由实验观测或者CFD模型计算而得）来训练搭建好的人工神经网络。 训练好的人工神经网络可以快速预测出任意工况下的多物理场分布，能够有效地节省计算资源。 通过比较相同工况下神经网络预测的结果和CFD模型计算的结果，我们发现神经网络预测结果的偏差最大不超过25%，最小仅为3%，具有较高的预测精度。 较传统的CFD模型而言，神经网络计算一组工况能在1s内完成（传统CFD模型计算一组工况需要8-12小时）。

Innovation

The data-driven surrogate model has a comparable accuracy to the comprehensive 3D physical model; however, it considerably reduces the cost of computation and time and achieves the efficient multi-physics-resolved digital-twin.

Experience Methods

In this model, an ANN is employed to predict the distributions of the physical quantities within the selected X–Z planes. The feed-forward multilayer perceptron is employed, and the Levenberg–Marquardt algorithmis used as the training function

Advantages

When the surrogate model is well-trained, the computational time of the surrogate model to predict the multi-physics fields in one operating condition scenario is wthin 1 s, whereas the that of one physical model case is approximately8–12 h by a four-processes parallel calculation.

Aplication Prospect

Developing a digital twin of a PEMFC, i.e. constructing a precise and virtual copy, to efficiently determine and monitor its multi-physics field state, will be of tremendous significance in cell design and control operation.  

Results and conclusion

In this study, we propose a data-driven surrogate modelling framework to achieve the multi-physics-resolved digital twin of PEMFCs. Simulation results of a state-of-the-art 3D physical model of PEMFCs construct a dataset and are applied to train the surrogate model. Two datadriven modelling methods with different data structures using ANN and SVM, respectively, are presented and compared. The prediction results show that the rRMSE in the test set of all the multi-physics fields range from 3.88% to 24.80%, and the prediction performance of the different physical quantities differs highly.
